The science of dough

Most of us know the ingredients that goes into making a dough but have we ever thought how does it work? 🤔

Key components and processes:

Flour

Flour is the primary source of proteins (gliadin and glutenin) that form gluten.

Water

Water hydrates the proteins, allowing them to interact and form gluten.

Gluten

Gluten is a protein network formed by gliadin and glutenin that gives dough its elasticity and ability too trap gases,

Kneading

Kneading develops gluten strength and elasticity by repeatedly severing and re-establishing disulfide bonds between glutenin molecules and it also warms the dough, which helps the proteins expand and bond further. The gluten network provides the dough with its ability to stretch and expand, which is essential for trapping gases during fermentation

In short, kneading transform the dough from a sticky, unorganised mass into a smooth, elastic ball.

Yeast

Yeast consumes sugar in the flour and produces carbon dioxide which are then trapped by the elastic gluten, causing the dough to expand and rise. Yeast also contributes to the characteristic flavors and aromas of bread through fermentation.

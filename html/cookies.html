Some pro tips to up your cookie game!

Use a scale instead of measuring cups.

Scales gives a more accurate measurement and it is consistent every time. When using measuring cups, there is the hassle of scooping and leveling the flour. Depending on the scoopping and leveling you might have an excess of flour or less flour than you actually need in your recipe.

Use room temperature ingredients, especially butter!

Remove the butter from the fridge atleast an hour before you start preparing. The butter must still be firm and not greasy. Bear in mind that the butter must not have been left out all day and too soft.

Chilling the cookie dough

If the recipe asks for it, then be a little patient and allow the dough to chill as it improves the texture and flavour of your cookies.

Cream the butter and sugar correctly!

The creaming process aerates the mixture that help leaven the cookie when it bakes. Creaming for too long can cause the cookie to puff too much and become cakey, creaming for not long enough may result in grainy cookies that don't puff or spread.
